About the Periodic Table

The periodic table is a tabular arrangement of the chemical elements, organized by their atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties. It was developed by Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev in 1869, who is credited with its first publication.

Element Organization

Elements are arranged in order of increasing atomic number and grouped by similar properties.

Periodic Trends

The table reveals patterns in element properties like atomic radius and electronegativity.

Chemical Prediction

Mendeleev's table predicted properties of then-unknown elements with remarkable accuracy.

Historical Development

1869

Mendeleev's Periodic Table

Dmitri Mendeleev published the first recognizable periodic table

1913

Atomic Number Organization

Henry Moseley arranged elements by atomic number rather than atomic weight

1940

Synthetic Elements

First synthetic elements (neptunium and plutonium) were discovered

2016

Complete 7th Period

The 7th period was completed with the addition of nihonium, moscovium, tennessine, and oganesson
